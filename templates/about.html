About the Health Risk and Resiliency Profile Project

This application extrapolates the CDC's existing efforts to better understand the overall health of Americans
and identify those biopsychosocial factors that may be contributing to risk for developing chronic illnesses.

The Health Risk and Resiliency Profile Project allows the general public to interface with the
Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System (BRFSS) dataset in a personally meaningful, intuitive way.

It aims to increase awareness of health disparaging and health promoting behaviors that can be modified
to improve specific health outomes and general wellness.

Predictions are generated using a supervised multivariate logistic regression ML model
and a KModes classification model for generating Health Behavior Archetypes.
SMOTE analysis was employed to adjust for unbalanced diagnostic groups.

Data is sourced from the 2016 CDC BRFSS database at: CDC Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System
